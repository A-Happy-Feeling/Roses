The Many Uses of Roses

Roses in Food & Drink

Roses are not just decorative — they are also edible. Rose petals and rose water are used in a variety of foods and drinks around the world. For example, rose lassi is a refreshing yoghurt-based drink, while rose-flavoured tea and desserts add a light floral taste. Rose petal shortbread is another example, combining sweetness with a subtle fragrance.

These uses show how roses can be enjoyed not only visually, but also through taste and smell.

Roses in Medicine

In addition to beauty, roses have also been used in traditional medicine. They are believed to have calming and anti-inflammatory properties. Rose-based remedies have been used to support digestion, reduce stress, and promote relaxation.

Although not all uses are scientifically proven, roses have long been valued for their potential health benefits.

Roses in Beauty & Skincare

Roses have been used in beauty products for thousands of years. Rose oil and rose water are commonly found in skincare because they are believed to help hydrate and soothe the skin. Many people also enjoy the natural fragrance of roses, which adds to their appeal in perfumes and cosmetics.

Because of these qualities, roses continue to be widely used in modern beauty products.
